    • Reasons for confusion:
      • Last week I was an expert. This week I don't know a thing. So this will be more a discussion than a presentation.
      • People understand things by example and analogy, so they try to translate the concepts to familiar terms such as javascript.
      • Javascript is weakly typed and strict. The use of monads in programming was introduced initially in Haskell, which is very strongly typed and lazy. Some of the reasons for using monads are not compelling in Javascript, and not even needed in a strict functional language such as Erlang.
      • Once people understand the concepts in the familiar environment they can see uses for them. However, they may still be unfamiliar with the reasons they were devised to begin with. (I am thinking of Crockford, here.)
      • The concepts don't directly correspond simply to language features. They are more like an algebra or a design pattern. Wadler refers to them as a "strategy." Monads are not so much a thing as formal characteristics of a number of things.
